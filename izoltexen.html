Uncoated Fabrics

The IZOLTEX fabrics are designed for insulation of hot surfaces with temperatures over +1200 C.

Tapes

Heat insulating and fireproof textile tapes have the same composition and intertwining as fabrics. They are designed for hot and cold surfaces insulation, as well as for fixed junctions sealing.

The IZOLTEX tapes are offered with or without an adhesive layer. The adhesive layer is applied on one of the tapes sides and protected with a thin layer of paper. The paper layer is removed when the tape is applied and the tape is easily fixed on the insulating surface.

Fabrics with Coatings

The IZOLTEX cloths with coatings consist of a high temperature warp and a coating made from heat-resistant silicon.

The coating is applied on one or both sides.

The coating features:
- protection of sleeve from external environment effects, soakings
- resistance to molten metal splashes
- protection from punctures, cuts and abrasion
- great chemical resistance dielectric features

Temperature parameters

The silicon coating can perform well under constant temperatures up to +300 C.

The coating is able to withstand temperatures up to +1200 C under short-term temperature jump conditions, depending on the process duration. For example, cloths with coatings performed well during a contact with molten metal splashes.

Reflective Coated Fabrics

Cloths covered with metal foil are able to protect from infrared radiation heat.

These cloths do not only reflect infrared radiation, but also are able to protect the insulating equipment’s surface from direct fire in case of a critical temperature increase and loss of the reflective layer.
